Trust Technologies

DTC Singapore Main Scope Of Trust Technologies

Trusted Analysis

The main focus will be related to the sharing of sensitive data in artificial intelligence and machine learning. The emphasis will be on trust-enhanced computational techniques that enable collaborative data analysis and model training without disclosing the actual raw data or the analytic model.

The proposed opportunities and focus areas shall include, but not limited to, the following:

  • Searchable Symmetric Encryption (SSE) has two notable limitations: i) it can leak user access and search patterns to servers and ii) it often lacks the necessary complex query expressiveness required for efficient and flexible search functionality on encrypted data.
  • Full Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) faces significant performance challenges due to the complexity of its algorithms and the substantial size of the ciphertexts. These factors result in high computational intensity, considerable communication overhead, and increased latency.
  • Private Set Intersection (PSI) encounters several challenges: i) performance and scalability issues become pronounced as the number of participating parties grows, and ii) the technology offers limited expressive queries and functionalities, such as the lack of support for range, threshold, and conjunctive queries.
  • Multi-Party Computation (MPC) faces significant practicability and scalability issues due to the communication and computational intensity of its protocols. These challenges are particularly pronounced when dealing with a large number of parties or complex computations.
  • Differential Privacy (DP) presents a crucial challenge in striking an optimal balance between privacy and utility when applying Differential Privacy.
  • Federated Learning (FL) poses the challenge of developing effective mechanisms for collaborative model selection, model aggregation, and model update strategies in Federated Learning. The goal is to minimize the impact on training accuracy.
  • Data Synthesis (DS) presents several critical challenges: handling complex data types, capturing spatio-temporal dependencies, and incorporating privacy-preserving mechanisms into the synthesis process.

Trusted Digital ID and Web 3.0

The focus will be on verifiable credentials and documents, and transferable records in decentralized environments. Additionally, it will cover track and trace mechanisms in supply chain management, digital identity solutions, and advanced analytics in decentralized finance.

The proposed opportunities and focus areas shall include, but not limited to, the following:

  • Trusted Digital Identity Network with emphasis on credentials and verification for digital ID, digital wallet and credential management, and digital ID network reference architecture. Key considerations include:

    1. What efficient data structures, cryptographic techniques, and distributed systems should be used to support scalable credential creation and verification.
    2. (i) What secure encryption techniques, access control mechanisms, and secure hardware solutions can safeguard the digital wallet contents, (ii) how techniques such as zero-knowledge proofs, selective disclosure mechanisms, and privacy-preserving authentication protocols can be used for credential governance in digital wallet.
    3. How to develop ontologies, metadata standards, and semantic mapping techniques that enable identity attributes and credentials to interoperate and be exchanged across multiple Digital ID networks.

  • Tokenisation and Token Standards for digital assets to support security and auditing. Key consideration include:

    1. How to develop decentralized tokenization systems that can effectively handle the blacklisting and revocation of tokens without compromising the core principles of decentralisation, immutability, and transparency.
    2. How to ensure transparent and auditable processes throughout the lifecycle of token.

  • Biometric-Assisted Authentication in Web 3.0 Key considerations include:

    1. How standardisation frameworks, interoperability protocols, and cross-platform libraries should be designed to support seamless integration and consistent performance of biometric authentication methods across diverse Web 3.0 environments.

  • Trust in Web 3.0 applications through blockchain traceability and analytics, risk assessments and verification of smart contracts against legal contracts , the use of self-sovereign identity (SSI). Key considerations include:

    1. How to develop formal verification techniques that can handle the features and constructs specific to smart contract languages, including contract invariants, authentication requirements, and access control policies.
    2. How to bridge the semantic gap between legal contract and smart contract by developing methods for interpreting legal texts, identifying contact terms and obligations, and mapping them to the corresponding smart contract logic.

Trusted Compute

The focus will be on how trusted execution environments (TEE) will impact the design and analysis of trusted applications. Such impacts include the specialized knowledge and understanding of the security features and programming models offered by specific TEE platforms. Furthermore, ensuring data provenance and providing verifiable evidence of the computation processes will be crucial for enhancing trust in the computation.

The proposed opportunities and focus areas shall include, but not limited to, the following:

  • Design and analysis of system environments for trusted computebased on applications of Trusted Execution Environments (TEE). Key considerations include:

    1. How to reduce computational complexity of privacy-enhanced algorithms when executed in resource-constrained TEE.
    2. How to design and execute cryptographic primitives such one-way hash function, digital signature) for privacy-preserving algorithms that are specially tailored for TEE

  • Verifiable Computing (VC) ensures correctness in distributed, decentralized infrastructure for specific computation tasks, such as e-voting tally. Key considerations include:

    1. How to develop more efficient and scalable VC protocols that minimise the computational and communication overhead and protect sensitive data during verification process.
    2. How to develop non-interactive protocols that reduce the asynchronous communication complexity between verifiers and provers from multiple servers.

  • Digital Evidence borrows digital forensic concepts and techniques that are relevant in a holistic approach to digital trust. This is because an important part of digital trust is the ability to establish accountability and liability in cases of fraud, transaction failures, or abnormal behaviour. Key considerations include:

    1. How to analyse cross-chain transactions and tracing assets across different blockchains, making use of interoperability protocols.
    2. How to identify and link real-world identities to blockchain addresses, enabling more transparent and accountable transactions.

Trusted Accreditation

The focus will be on trusted AI model testing, encompassing: (i) Research into scientific techniques for evaluating key aspects such as fairness, explainability, robustness, and hallucination, ensuring that AI models are reliable and ethical. (ii) For various AI and Machine Learning (ML) models, encompassing supervised and unsupervised learning as well as generative and non-generative AI, with respect to the following challenges.

  • Hallucination – How to address factually inaccurate or fake outputs in Generative AI. Key considerations include:

    1. How to develop scientific methods in order to reduce hallucinations during decoding and pre-empt incorrect associations learned.
    2. How to fine-tune or distil strategies to train models so as to gain the accuracy of the output.
    3. How to develop technical strategies to detect hallucinations in output and check output with sources of factuality.
    4. How to develop ground truth / benchmark data , and evaluation metrics to objectively assess the amount and types of hallucinations.

  • Fairness– How to make sure no unintended bias involves AI systems. This is because AI system makes same decision even if an attribute changes, and data used to train model is representative. Key considerations include:

    1. How to develop new testing algorithms for fairness in different types of data for binary and multi-class classification and regression models.
    2. How to develop fairness metrics and break down bias in different applications and use cases.
    3. How to mitigate bias and assess techniques that handle multi-lingual input and generated output.
    4. How to investigate bias at data processing and learning state in order to address any imbalances.
    5. How to benchmark data to measure degree of social biases in Singapore/Asian contexts.

  • Explainability– How to explain behaviour of AI models and/or multi-modal models in order to understand how the inner mechanics impact the generated output. The examples of problem include lack of transparency, non-deterministic outputs, high dimensionality, and overfitting. Key considerations include:

    1. How to develop new techniques to understand local vs global model structure for binary and multi-class classification and regression models in (i) different types of data, and (ii) multi-modal AI models that correlate results from individual components.
    2. How to research into different architectures such as neural network architecture that support explainability.
    3. How to develop attribution methods to identify important training instances used to create better training data to fine-tune AI models.

  • Robustness – How to address issues related to robustness, including non-adversarial robustness and privacy-related attacks. Key considerations include:

    1. How to develop new measures to detect non-adversarial examples on binary and multi-class classification and regression models and in different types of data, and to demonstrate how remediation can be done.
    2. How to develop robustness metrics to quantify and new methods to improve non-adversarial robustness.
    3. How to build and maintain foundation models with data protection and privacy-enhancing technologies.
    4. How to benchmark different data used for robustness testing.
