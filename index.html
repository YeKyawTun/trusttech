About Us

Welcome to the Body of Knowledge, presented by The Digital Trust Centre Singapore (DTC). Our commitment is to enhance digital trust and drive innovation across various sectors. Central to our mission is fostering collaboration, enabling effective networking, and promoting the exchange of invaluable knowledge within the trust technology landscape.

The Body of Knowledge (BOK) supports the development of TrustTech by linking IHLs and industry stakeholders, fostering collaboration, and promoting innovation. It aims to educate and inform on all issues relating to digital trust, promoting a deeper understanding and wider adoption of trust technologies.

Central to our platform is the ‘Digital Directory’, functioning like a "Yellow Pages" for Institutes of Higher Learning (IHLs) and industry players focused on trust technology. This vibrant hub allows you to discover solutions, express your needs, and engage with a community rich in expertise.

Our ambition is to cultivate a strong digital trust community by establishing a robust and smoothly connected nationwide network where stakeholders continuously collaborate, communicate, and innovate, all dedicated to advancing digital trust.

Join our dynamic network today and play a part in shaping a secure and innovative digital future.

Why do we need Trust Technologies?

Digital trust is essential for both organizations and consumers, requiring companies to prove their authenticity and secure customer data through robust technology solutions. This trust is crucial for maintaining secure online relationships and the adoption of online and e-government services. As digital practices evolve, businesses and governments must implement secure technologies and foster a culture of transparency and innovation.

Key points:

  • Digital trust is vital for secure online relationships and the adoption of services.
  • Privacy and data management are crucial concerns.
  • Secure technologies and transparency are essential for trust.
  • Innovation and collaboration enhance trust and security.
  • Trust technologies improve security measures and operational transparency.

The Trust Technology Strategic Plan

The Trust Technology Strategic Plan proposed by Digital Trust Centre, Singapore outlines a comprehensive framework for advancing trust in technology through paradigm, foundational research, technological development, and applications. The plan is divided into several key sections:

Paradigm: This section focuses on the overarching concepts that drive trust in technology, including Perturbation, Cryptography, Decentralization, and Trustworthy Computing

Foundational Research: Underpinning the paradigms are crucial research areas such as Differential Privacy (DP), Searchable Symmetric Encryption (SSE), Full Homomorphic Encryption (FHE), Multi-Party Computation & Private Set Intersection (MPC & PSI), Federated Learning (FL), Blockchain, Digital Identity (Digital ID), Trusted Execution Environment (TEE), Verifiable Compute, Digital Forensics, and Fair, Non-Hallucination, Robust and Explainable AI.

Trust Technologies: Building on the research, the plan identifies key technologies such as Privacy-Preserving Technology (PP-Tech), Web 3.0, and Trusted Digital ID, Trusted Machine Learning and AI Testing.

  • Privacy-Preserving Technologies can be built by utilizing the foundational research such as Differential Privacy, Searchable Symmetric Encryption, Full Homomorphic Encryption, Multi-Party Computation & Private Set Intersection, and Federated Learning.
  • Trusted Digital ID and Web 3.0 can be implemented with baseline research such as Blockchain and Digital ID.
  • Trusted Machine Learning can be deployed with core research including Full Homomorphic Encryption, Multi-Party Computation & Private Set Intersection, Federated Learning, and Fairness, Non-hallucination, Robustness and Explainability of AI Models.
  • AI Testing can be carried out using the scientific techniques focusing on fairness, non-hallucination, robustness and explainable.
  • Trusted Analysis can be composed of technologies, especially driven from Privacy-Preserving Technologies.
  • Trusted Applications can be developed by using both Web 3.0 and Trusted Digital ID.
  • Trusted Compute can be engineered using technologies such as Multi-Party Computation & Private Set Intersection, Trusted Execution Environment, Verifiable Compute, and Digital Forensic.
  • Trusted Accreditation can be built from Trusted Machine Learning and AI Testing.
