Who We Are

Welcome to the Body of Knowledge, presented by The Digital Trust Centre Singapore (DTC). Our commitment is to enhance digital trust and drive innovation across various sectors. Central to our mission is fostering collaboration, enabling effective networking, and promoting the exchange of invaluable knowledge within the trust technology landscape.

The Body of Knowledge (BOK) supports the development of TrustTech by linking IHLs and industry stakeholders, fostering collaboration, and promoting innovation. It aims to educate and inform on all issues relating to digital trust, promoting a deeper understanding and wider adoption of trust technologies.

Central to our platform is the ‘Digital Directory’, functioning like a "Yellow Pages" for Institutes of Higher Learning (IHLs) and industry players focused on trust technology. This vibrant hub allows you to discover solutions, express your needs, and engage with a community rich in expertise.

Our ambition is to cultivate a strong digital trust community by establishing a robust and smoothly connected nationwide network where stakeholders continuously collaborate, communicate, and innovate, all dedicated to advancing digital trust.

Join our dynamic network today and play a part in shaping a secure and innovative digital future.

What are Trust Technologies?

Digital trust is essential for both organizations and consumers, requiring companies to prove their authenticity and secure customer data through robust technology solutions. This trust is crucial for maintaining secure online relationships and the adoption of online and e-government services. As digital practices evolve, businesses and governments must implement secure technologies and foster a culture of transparency and innovation.

Key points:

  • Digital trust is vital for secure online relationships and the adoption of services.
  • Privacy and data management are crucial concerns.
  • Secure technologies and transparency are essential for trust.
  • Innovation and collaboration enhance trust and security.
  • Trust technologies improve security measures and operational transparency.

  • Focuses on enhancing data privacy and security
  • Uses techniques like Searchable Symmetric Encryption (SSE), Full Homomorphic Encryption (FHE), Private Set Intersection (PSI), Multi-Party Computation (MPC), Differential Privacy (DP), Federated Learning (FL), Data Synthesis (DS)
  • Enables secure data analysis without compromising information

  • Emphasies securing computational environments
  • Involves Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs), data provenance, verifiable computing, and digital forensis
  • Aims to safeguard processes and ensure trustworthy outcomes

  • Focuses on securing digital identities in a decentralized web
  • Includes Trusted Digital Identity Network, tokenization, biometric authentication
  • Aims for privacy-centric, user-controlled digital identities

  • Validates fairness, understandability, and robustness of AI/ML models
  • Addresses challenges like bias, opacity, and vulnerability to attacks
  • Rigorous testing ensures adherence to ethical standards

  • Designed to address emerging and cross-disciplinary challenges in trust and technology
  • Covers innovative technologies, providing flexibility for novel solutions
  • Fosters advancements in trust and security across all aspects of digital technology
