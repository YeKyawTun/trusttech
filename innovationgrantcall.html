Innovation Grant Call

The DTC Singapore Innovation Grant Call is a research funding program designed to foster collaboration between Singapore-based Institutes of Higher Learning (IHLs) / Research Institutes (RIs) and companies operating in Singapore.

The aim is to jointly develop Trust Tech solutions that drive innovation, support digital transformation, and translate these advancements into practical applications. To qualify for the grant, IHLs/RIs must partner with companies to create multi-disciplinary teams or consortia comprising industry experts, engineers, researchers, scientists, academics and other professionals.

For the DTC Singapore Innovation Grant Call, the timeline was displayed below:

Scope of Innovation Grant Call

The proposed opportunities and areas of focus may encompass, but are not limited to, the following:

The primary focus will involve the sharing of sensitive data and models in both artificial intelligence and machine learning. The emphasis will be on utilizing trust technologies that enable computation without revealing the underlying raw data or analytic models, such as:

  • Privacy-preserving AI
  • Privacy-preserving Technologies
    • Full Homomorphic Encryption (FHE)
    • Searchable Symmetric Encryption (SSE) with analytics
    • Data Synthesis (DS)
    • Secure Multi-Party Computation (SMPC)
    • Federated Learning (FL)
    • Differential Privacy (DP)

The emphasis will be on verifiable credentials and documents, transferable records within a decentralized environment, supply chain tracking and tracing, digital identity, and analytics in decentralized finance.

  • Blockchain analytics (in particular, against financial crimes)
  • Trusted decentralised digital identity
  • Decentralized computing and storage systems

The emphasis will be on: (i) foundational AI model risks affecting the entire AI ecosystem, (ii) services and platforms available for conducting foundational AI model risk assessments, and (iii) strategies to mitigate AI risks or minimize the impact of potential failures across the AI ecosystem.

  • Trustworthy AI
    • Function as intended
    • No authorized modifications
    • Operated in a secure manner
  • Responsible AI – Traditional AI risks of users and organizations
    • Fair, Transparent, Explainable
    • Accountable, ethically developed
    • No misuse of data in favor of ML
  • Safe AI – Frontier AI risks of societal harms and public safety
    • Harmful content, Value Alignment
    • Disinformation, Hallucination
    • Copyright, Data supply chain risk
