Research Grant Call

Research Grant Call

The DTC Singapore Research Grant Call is a research funding program designed to support research projects that contribute to the advancement of trust technologies science across Singapore-based Institutes of Higher Learning (IHLs) and Research Institutes (RIs).

For the DTC Singapore Research Grant Call, the timeline was displayed below:

Event Date
Opening date for submission (softcopy via email) 30 June 2023
Submission deadline 30 August 2023, 5:00 pm
Evaluation and approval of proposals August to October 2023
Release of outcome and award 30 November 2023
Project commencement December 2023 onwards

Scope of Research Grant Call

The proposed opportunities and areas of focus may encompass, but are not limited to, the following:

The central focus will be on the exchange of sensitive data in the context of artificial intelligence and machine learning. The priority will be on employing trust technologies to perform computations without exposing the raw data or the analytical models.

  • Searchable Symmetric Encryption (SSE)
  • Full Homomorphic Encryption (FHE)
  • Private Set Intersection (PSI)
  • Multi-Party Computation (MPC)
  • Differential Privacy (DP)
  • Federated Learning (FL)
  • Data Synthesis (DS)

The focus will be on verifiable credentials and documents, transferable records in decentralized settings, supply chain tracking and tracing, digital identity, and analytics in decentralized finance.

  • Trusted Digital Identity Network
  • Tokenization
  • Biometric-assisted authentication
  • Trust in Web 3.0 applications

The emphasis will be on testing trusted AI models, including: (i) researching scientific methods for evaluating aspects such as fairness, explainability, robustness, and hallucination, and (ii) addressing challenges related to different AI and Machine Learning (ML) models, including both supervised and unsupervised learning, as well as non-generative and non-generative AI.

The emphasis will be on: (i) foundational AI model risks affecting the entire AI ecosystem, (ii) services and platforms available for conducting foundational AI model risk assessments, and (iii) strategies to mitigate AI risks or minimize the impact of potential failures across the AI ecosystem. The certain challenges are outlined below

  • Hallucination
  • Fairness
  • Explainability
  • Robustness
